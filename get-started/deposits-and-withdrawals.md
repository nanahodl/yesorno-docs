# Deposits & Withdrawals

Yes/No uses **USDC** as the settlement currency for every trade, deposit, and payout. Deposit USDC to start trading, and withdraw anytime back to your external wallet.

## Deposit Flow

![](../assets/diagrams/get-started_deposits-and-withdrawals_1.svg)

Regardless of how you signed in (crypto wallet or email), you deposit USDC into your Yes/No account before trading.

1. Open **Wallet → Deposit**
2. Copy your Yes/No deposit address
3. Send USDC to that address from an external wallet or exchange
4. Wait for on-chain confirmation — USDC is then credited to your account

## Supported Networks

USDC deposits are accepted on the networks listed on the **Deposit** page. Sending USDC from an unsupported network **will result in permanent loss of funds**.

{% hint style="danger" %}
Always confirm the network before sending. Tokens sent on the wrong chain cannot be recovered.
{% endhint %}

## Minimums

| Action | Minimum |
| --- | --- |
| Deposit USDC | 1 USDC |
| Withdraw USDC | 1 USDC |

Standard network gas applies to on-chain transactions.

## Withdrawal Flow

![](../assets/diagrams/get-started_deposits-and-withdrawals_2.svg)

### Steps

1. Open **Wallet → Withdraw**
2. Enter the destination address and the amount
3. Confirm the network is correct
4. Confirm the withdrawal

Most withdrawals process within a few minutes. Final settlement depends on the network's on-chain confirmation time.

## Troubleshooting

### Deposit not showing up

1. Confirm the transaction was sent on a **supported network**
2. Wait for the required on-chain confirmations
3. Check the transaction on the block explorer using the hash
4. If missing after 30 minutes, contact **support@yesorno.trade** with the transaction hash

### Withdrawal is pending

Large or unusual withdrawals may be held briefly for manual review. You'll receive an email update if any action is needed from your side.

### Sent to wrong network

Funds sent on an unsupported network are generally **unrecoverable**. Always verify the network indicator on the Deposit page before sending.

## Security

* Double-check the destination address before confirming
* Use the network indicator on the Deposit page — not third-party sources
* Yes/No will **never** DM you asking for a "verification" transaction

For more, see [Sign Up & Wallet → Account Security](sign-up-and-wallet.md#account-security).
