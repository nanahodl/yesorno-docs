# Refund Policy

## Overview

Yes/No is a peer-to-peer market. When you trade, you're buying shares from or selling them to other users. Prices move based on supply and demand, and you take on the market risk of your position. **Normal trading losses are not refundable.**

That said, there are specific circumstances where a market may be voided and affected positions refunded.

## When Markets May Be Voided

A market may be voided when:

* The resolution source becomes unavailable and no acceptable alternative exists
* The event is cancelled or indefinitely postponed with no clear path to resolution
* The outcome is genuinely ambiguous and cannot be determined fairly under the rules
* A critical error is discovered in the market's setup that materially affects fairness

If a void is declared, Yes/No will announce the decision on the affected market page and notify holders. The specific handling of any refund is determined on a case-by-case basis in accordance with the [Terms of Use](terms-of-service.md).

## What Is Not Refundable

* Normal trading losses (you were on the wrong side of a market that resolved correctly)
* Losses from slippage on market orders
* Losses from orders you placed by mistake
* Losses from price movements while your order was open
* Network gas fees paid to the blockchain

## Appealing a Resolution

If you believe a market resolved incorrectly against its published rules:

1. Email **support@yesorno.trade** with the market name and your concern
2. Include any supporting evidence (official sources, timestamps, etc.)
3. Appeals are reviewed against the market's published rules

{% hint style="warning" %}
Resolutions consistent with the published rules are **final**, even if you disagree with the real-world outcome. See the [Terms of Use](terms-of-service.md) for the binding dispute resolution procedure.
{% endhint %}
